Upper Unionville Golf Club

Rating: 4.1/5

Phone number: (905) 888-8842

Location: 11207 Kennedy Road, Markham ON L6C 1P2

Course website: Upper Unionville Golf Club

Scorecard: Upper Unionville

General description: Formerly known as The Mandarin Golf & Country Club in 1991, this private-turned-public golf course was renamed as the Upper Unionville Golf Club under the Copper Creek Golf Group (CCGG). The CCGG offers a flex membership which provides player benefits to four courses: Upper Unionville, Copper Creek, Watson's Glen, and Peninsula Lakes. This course was designed by Doug Carrick, who maintained the classic style of 1920's and 30's golf courses, while adding a modern twist to it. The fairways are generous and the greens are on the softer side, however, they are surrounded by strategically placed bunkers.

Review: When I first played at Upper Unionville, the fairways felt exceptionally spacious and nicely cut, and the greens rolled at a fine speed; it was not too firm nor too soft. The course challenges your course management skills by requiring a good aim and distance to find the fairways, as it is filled with doglegs ranging from a slight, smooth bend to a near 90-degree curve around a pond or a dense forest. I enjoyed taking on Upper Unionville's par 3s, as each of them were beautifully designed with a unique degree of difficulty using a variety of elements.

I found the most remarkable hole of the course to be its island green on the 16th hole (as shown in the photo above), boasting a magnificent view of the green surrounded on all sides by a daunting lake. The lake is home to the balls of countless unlucky golfers and a few toads that may be spotted as you cross the bridge to the putting green. In the recent years I have revisited this course, I noticed that the texture of the fairways have gotten dry and patchy. Nonetheless, Upper Unionville is always a pleasure to play at, and I would recommend this course if you are looking for something moderately challenging with decent-sized fairways.
