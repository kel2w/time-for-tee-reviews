North Course at Angus Glen Golf Club

Rating: 4.4/5

Phone number: (905) 887-0090

Location: 10080 Kennedy Road, Markham ON L6C 1N9

Course website: Angus Glen Golf Club

Scorecard: North Course

General description: The North Course is one of the two 18-hole golf courses at the Angus Glen Golf Club known for hosting the Canadian Open, a PGA event, in 2007. The course offers four tees to play from, and maintains bentgrass greens and fairways bordered by fescue-covered dunes on each hole. Angus Glen Golf Club features an extravagant clubhouse that provides full dining and proshop services, GPS golf carts, and excellent practice facilities consisting of a driving range, two putting and chipping greens, and a bunker.

Review: Compared to the South Course, the North Course is rather forgiving with its wide-open landscape, and links-style course type originating from Scotland. It was satisfying to hit out of the rough and the fairways, as the grass was thick and well-groomed for a solid, clean strike. However, the majority of the fairways are heavily sloped with a tendency of the holes to end downhill. The difficulty of this course lies with distance control, especially on the downhill par 3s, and playing around the fast-rolling greens decorated with sod wall bunkers. Pictured above is Hole 13, depicting a picturesque view of the dogleg hole bending around an extensive lake. Visually, it is a mentally challenging hole, which is why it's my favourite hole on this course, as it is provides a fun challenge alongside a beautiful view.

I noticed that the North Course played slightly longer from the tees than the typical golf course in Ontario, though the occasional downhill slopes may compensate for the extra distance. Also, the holes are fairly spaced out, which requires a lengthier walk than usual to get to the next hole if you aren't taking carts. From my experience, the pacing of the course was usually unhurried given the generous amount of space between the holes, and the tendency of players to take power carts instead of walking on foot. Overall, I would definitely recommend this course for a less stimulating round of golf, due to its straightforward layout.
